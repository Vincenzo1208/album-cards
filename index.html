THOR

About Thor

Thor, also known as the God of Thunder, is a superhero in the Marvel Comics universe. He is based on the Norse mythology deity of the same name.

Thor is the son of Odin, the All-Father of Asgard, and possesses immense strength, the ability to control lightning and storms, and near-immortality.

About Hulk

The Hulk, also known as Dr. Bruce Banner, is a superhero in the Marvel Comics universe. He was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby and first appeared in "The Incredible Hulk" #1 in 1962.

Bruce Banner is a brilliant scientist who, after being exposed to gamma radiation during an experiment, transforms into the Hulk when he experiences extreme stress or anger.

About Loki

Loki, also known as the God of Mischief, is a supervillain and anti-hero in the Marvel Comics universe. He is based on the Norse mythology deity of the same name.

Loki is the adopted brother of Thor and the son of Laufey, the king of the Frost Giants.

Potrayed by

Loki serves as the main antagonist in "The Avengers" (2012) and has appeared in several other MCU films, including "Thor: The Dark World," "Thor: Ragnarok," and "Avengers: Infinity War" and "Avengers: Endgame." Loki's character arc in the MCU explores themes of redemption and identity.

About Valkyre

Valkyrie, also known as Brunnhilde, is a superhero in the Marvel Comics universe. She is one of the Valkyrior, a group of female warriors in Asgardian mythology who serve Odin by bringing the souls of fallen heroes to Valhalla.

Valkyrie possesses superhuman strength, stamina, and durability, making her a formidable combatant.
